# Creative Direction Seeds

Use these seeds to produce different layout skeletons quickly. Adapt them to the product. Do not copy them literally. Referenced from the Creative direction pass step in `SKILL.md`.

For each seed, borrow the composition logic first, styling second.

## How to use seeds
- Pick 2 or 3 that could plausibly fit the product.
- Force them to differ at the layout-skeleton level, not just tone.
- Seeds can be hybridized (see "Combining seeds" at the bottom).
- Reject any seed that fights the product's primary task.

## 1. Editorial Premium
Best for:
- premium consumer products
- storytelling pages
- launches, portfolios, polished landing pages

Example products: Apple product pages, Stripe announcements, Linear launch posts, Vercel marketing pages.

Layout skeleton:
- one dominant textual or visual anchor
- asymmetrical supporting content
- generous open space with a controlled scan path

Signature move:
- make one bold typographic or compositional anchor carry the first impression

Keep plain:
- secondary sections
- utility actions
- separators and backgrounds

Breakpoint note:
- preserve the anchor on mobile; do not reduce it to a generic stacked hero

## 2. Product as Instrument
Best for:
- dashboards
- devtools
- workflow products
- operational software

Example products: Linear, Figma, Notion, Raycast, Datadog, Sentry, Arc.

Layout skeleton:
- one dominant workspace
- one secondary rail or support zone
- commands and state changes near the work area

Signature move:
- make the screen feel like a tool the user operates, not a brochure made of cards

Keep plain:
- low-priority stats
- passive metadata
- chrome around secondary actions

Breakpoint note:
- decide what remains persistent on mobile and what becomes progressive disclosure

## 3. Calm Premium Utility
Best for:
- finance
- health
- productivity
- trust-heavy flows

Example products: Wise, Monzo, Wealthfront, Oura, Things, Bear.

Layout skeleton:
- large calm blocks
- restrained surfaces
- clear sequencing from explanation to action

Signature move:
- use calm spacing and sharp hierarchy instead of decoration to signal quality

Keep plain:
- icons
- backgrounds
- secondary surfaces

Breakpoint note:
- keep the calm hierarchy on small screens by reducing clutter before reducing type size

## 4. Technical Confidence
Best for:
- B2B products
- infrastructure
- security
- analytics and data-dense tools

Example products: Cloudflare dashboards, Grafana, Snowflake, HashiCorp docs and consoles.

Layout skeleton:
- precise alignment
- compact but ordered density
- strong labels and sectional hierarchy

Signature move:
- make the interface feel exact, capable, and trustworthy through structure

Keep plain:
- accent color usage
- ornament
- non-critical surfaces

Breakpoint note:
- preserve ordering and labels on mobile even if density drops sharply

## 5. Focused Conversion
Best for:
- signup pages
- upgrade flows
- launches
- conversion-focused marketing pages

Example products: pricing pages, trial-start flows, single-product landing pages, upgrade prompts.

Layout skeleton:
- one dominant conversion path
- proof placed near the main action
- fewer sections with stronger sequencing

Signature move:
- bring proof and product evidence closer to the CTA than marketing pages usually do

Keep plain:
- tertiary navigation
- decorative testimonials
- background treatments

Breakpoint note:
- keep CTA and proof visually adjacent on smaller screens instead of separating them into long stacks

## 6. Playful / Expressive
Best for:
- consumer apps with personality
- social, community, creative tools
- games, entertainment, kids products
- brand moments where warmth matters more than restraint

Example products: Duolingo, Discord, Figma community pages, Bandcamp, Are.na, creative-tool launch pages.

Layout skeleton:
- character-forward composition (illustration, mascot, bespoke typography, or expressive color block)
- intentional asymmetry and looser rhythm
- one warm, memorable detail that the rest of the page orbits around

Signature move:
- let personality do structural work — the expressive element is the focal anchor, not decoration bolted onto a generic layout

Keep plain:
- forms, settings, and utility flows inside the same product
- dense content views where expressiveness would get in the way
- secondary navigation

Breakpoint note:
- expressive moves must survive mobile without becoming cramped; if the mascot or bespoke type dies at `sm`, the direction is broken

Caution:
- playful does not mean juvenile; the test is whether the expressive element carries meaning or just vibes

## 7. Narrative Walkthrough
Best for:
- onboarding flows
- interactive docs and tutorials
- case studies, launch explainers
- long-form feature pages

Example products: Stripe docs walkthroughs, Linear changelog entries, Notion templates, Vercel guides, Rauno's scroll essays.

Layout skeleton:
- strong vertical sequence with deliberate pacing
- alternating density: explanation → artifact → explanation → artifact
- micro-anchors (step numbers, scroll markers, sticky context) that orient without dominating

Signature move:
- make scroll position itself carry meaning; each section earns its place by advancing the story

Keep plain:
- chrome and navigation
- repeated section frames (they are supporting, not competing)
- the ending — resist decorative finales, let the conclusion be quiet

Breakpoint note:
- mobile often strengthens narrative layouts; resist the urge to compress pacing into a single scroll of cards

## Combining seeds
Hybridization is encouraged when one pure seed does not fit the product. Common productive hybrids:

- **Product as Instrument + Calm Premium Utility** — operational tools in regulated industries (banking dashboards, health records). The workspace dominates, but ornament is stripped further than default Product-as-Instrument.
- **Editorial Premium + Focused Conversion** — premium launch pages where the first impression is editorial and the bottom of the page becomes a focused conversion pattern.
- **Technical Confidence + Narrative Walkthrough** — developer docs and API explainers. Precise typography and alignment on top of a paced narrative spine.
- **Playful / Expressive + Focused Conversion** — consumer signup flows where personality needs to survive alongside a clear conversion path.

Rule: when hybridizing, pick one seed as the dominant compositional logic and let the other contribute a single characteristic — tone, pacing, or density. Do not blend them 50/50; one has to win.
